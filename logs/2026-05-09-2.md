## 2026-05-09 — NestJS dependency injection

Man, DI in NestJS. Was just messing around with a new service and it hit me today how `providers: []` in modules actually *works*. Like, it's not just a list, it's Nest saying "hey, I'll manage this instance for you." And the default's a singleton, which I totally missed before. So when I inject `MyService` into a controller, Nest just hands me the *same one* every time. No need for weird factory patterns or anything manually. Just `@Injectable()` on the class, add it to providers, done. Wild.
