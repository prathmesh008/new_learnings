## 2026-04-30 — JWT vs sessions

Okay so been reading about JWT vs sessions again and wow, it finally clicked why JWTs are so... stateless. Like, with sessions, your server *has* to remember you, checking its own store for `sessionId: 'someUser'` every request. But JWT? Nah, you just send the token, `Authorization: Bearer <token>`, and the server just *trusts* it after crypto verification, no database lookup needed. That's the real magic for scale, not having to hold state for every damn user. Such a relief to finally get that.
