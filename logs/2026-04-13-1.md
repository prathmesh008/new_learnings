## 2026-04-13 — async/await under the hood

Okay, so turns out `async/await` isn't some black magic that lets JS suddenly do multithreading, lol. I always kinda imagined `await` was like a full-on thread block, but nah. What it actually does is more like... it just marks the spot where the `async` function *itself* should pause, then lets the event loop pick up other tasks while it waits for the `Promise` to resolve. So, `const data = await fetchData();` just registers a callback for `fetchData`'s promise. It's still single-threaded, just super smart about yielding control. Mind. Blown.
