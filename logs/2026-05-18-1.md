## 2026-05-18 — async/await under the hood

Okay so I was reading more about async/await today, beyond just "it makes async code look sync." Turns out when you hit an `await`, the function doesn't just sit there. The compiler basically rewrites your function into a state machine, like, it saves its current state and "pauses" the execution flow, yielding control back to the event loop. Then when the awaited promise resolves, the event loop picks it up from the *exact* spot it left off. That's actually pretty wild and explains why it's not blocking everything. Like, `await somePromise()` just packs up the current context and says "call me back later." Mind blown.
