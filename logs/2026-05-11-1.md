## 2026-05-11 — WebSocket internals

Okay so I was digging into WebSockets today, specifically how they even get *started*. Always kinda assumed they just... connected, but nope. The initial connection is actually just a regular old HTTP request! That was a mind-bender. Like, the client sends a `GET /chat HTTP/1.1` but with these special `Upgrade: websocket` and `Connection: Upgrade` headers. Server gets it, agrees, and then *poof*, it's no longer HTTP, it's a persistent, bidirectional WebSocket connection. Makes so much more sense now why you see that header. So neat how it transitions.
