## 2026-05-25 — async/await under the hood

Been reading about async/await under the hood today, and it finally properly clicked. Like, async functions? They *always* return a Promise, no matter what, even if you just `return 'hello'`. And `await` just tells *that specific function* to pause until the Promise settles, then it unwraps the value. So it's not like, blocking the whole event loop or anything, just *this* function's execution flow. It's just fancy Promise chaining syntax, honestly. Blew my mind a little how straightforward it is once you get past the magic.
