## 2026-04-29 — nginx reverse proxy

Okay so finally getting my head around nginx `proxy_pass`. Like, I always knew it forwarded requests but the *path* part was kinda fuzzy. If nginx listens on `/api` and then `proxy_pass http://localhost:3000/`, does my backend get `/api/foo` or just `/foo`? Turns out if you add the trailing slash to `proxy_pass`, it strips the matched location prefix. `location /api/ { proxy_pass http://localhost:3000/; }` so `/api/users` becomes `/users` for my node app. Makes so much sense for microservices now, total lightbulb moment.
