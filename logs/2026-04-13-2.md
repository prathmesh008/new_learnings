## 2026-04-13 — JavaScript closures

Man, closures. Today was the day it finally clicked, like, properly clicked. Always kinda *knew* what they were but didn't grok why they were so big. But seeing how a function remembers its environment, even after the outer one's long gone? That private counter example, `let count = 0; return function() { count++; return count; }`, that was it. The `count` just hangs around. Wild.
