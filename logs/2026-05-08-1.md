## 2026-05-08 — nginx reverse proxy

Okay so, Nginx reverse proxy stuff finally clicked a bit more today. I was messing with some local services and couldn't figure out why my Node app on port 3000 wasn't showing up when I hit port 80. Realized Nginx isn't *running* the app, it's just redirecting the traffic. The `proxy_pass http://localhost:3000;` line literally just tells Nginx, "hey, send whatever comes to me here, over to *that* address." Simple, but it was just a blur before. Makes so much more sense now.
