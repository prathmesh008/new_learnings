## 2026-04-16 — environment variables best practices

Okay, so I was messing around with env vars again today. The whole `.env` thing for local dev makes sense now, like, it's *just* for local stuff, right? What really clicked is that you absolutely, positively do NOT commit that file. It's for secrets and configs that change between dev, test, prod. For prod, you set them directly on the server or in your hosting platform. Like accessing `process.env.API_KEY` in Node. Super important to keep them out of git, otherwise your secrets are just... out there. Seems obvious now but confused me a while back.
