## 2026-05-28 — NestJS dependency injection

Okay so been poking at NestJS DI again. Man, the whole `@Injectable()` thing finally clicked, mostly. For ages I was like, why *also* list it in the `providers` array in the module? Isn't `@Injectable()` enough? Turns out `@Injectable()` just flags a class that *can* be injected, but the `providers: [UsersService]` part is what actually tells Nest to *create an instance* and make it available. So glad that finally made sense lol. Felt kinda dumb for not getting that sooner, but hey, progress.
