## 2026-05-23 — NestJS dependency injection

Okay, so NestJS DI clicked a bit more today. I was messing around with a new `UserService`, and just, like, putting `private readonly userService: UserService` in the controller's constructor. It just *works*. No manual `new UserService()` anywhere, which is wild. Nest figures that out automatically, as long as `UserService` is `@Injectable()` and listed as a provider in the module, y'know, `providers: [UserService]`. That's it. Super clean, much less boilerplate.
