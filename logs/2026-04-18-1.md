## 2026-04-18 — PostgreSQL indexing

Okay so I was digging into Postgres indexing today and it finally clicked why B-tree is the default. Like, you can write `CREATE INDEX ON users (email) USING BTREE;` but that `USING BTREE` part is basically redundant, you never really need it for standard stuff. It's just so damn good for equality checks and range queries like `WHERE created_at BETWEEN '...' AND '...'`. My `EXPLAIN ANALYZE` for a simple `SELECT * FROM products WHERE category_id = 5` went from sequential scan to index scan instantly once I added one. Makes so much sense.
