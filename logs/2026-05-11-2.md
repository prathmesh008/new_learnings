## 2026-05-11 — React rendering optimization

Okay so `React.memo` just clicked for me. I always kinda knew it was for performance but today it properly sunk in how it stops child components from re-rendering when their props haven't *actually* changed. It's like, so simple. Just wrap your component export: `export default React.memo(MyComponent);`. Duh, why did that take me so long to grasp? So much less unnecessary work for React.
