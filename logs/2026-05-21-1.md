## 2026-05-21 — async/await under the hood

okay so been diving into async/await, and man, the event loop stuff really clicked today. `await` doesn't just pause, it literally *suspends* the current async function, adds the rest of its execution as a 'task' to the microtask queue once the promise settles, and lets other code run. like, it's not blocking the main thread at all. mind = blown. always thought it was some kinda magic wait until now, but it's just really smart task scheduling.
