## 2026-05-05 — Docker volumes

Man, volumes. They always felt kinda abstract but today it just clicked. That difference between bind mounts and named volumes? Massive. Named ones are like, completely Docker's baby, it handles where they actually live on disk, which is super neat for persistence across container restarts or even deleting them. Bind mounts are just... "here's a folder from my laptop, container, use it." Like `docker run -d -v my-named-vol:/app/data my-image` for the named kind. Makes way more sense now why you'd pick one over the other.
