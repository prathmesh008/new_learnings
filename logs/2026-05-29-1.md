## 2026-05-29 — async/await under the hood

Okay so, been digging into `async/await` under the hood today and wow. It really just *pauses* that *specific* async function until the awaited promise settles, not the whole damn thread. That was a big "duh" moment for me, cuz it just feels so synchronous when you write it. It's literally just sugar over `.then()` callbacks, huh? So like, when `await somePromise;` hits, the runtime just squirrels away the rest of the function and lets the event loop do its thing. Totally changes how I think about concurrency now.
