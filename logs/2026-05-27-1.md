## 2026-05-27 — NestJS dependency injection

Okay so, been reading about NestJS DI again, specifically the whole `@Injectable()` thing. For ages, I just slapped it on a service and expected magic. But today it really clicked that `@Injectable()` just *marks* the class. The actual magic, the part where Nest knows how to *create* and *provide* it, happens in the module's `providers` array. Like, if you forget `providers: [MyService]` in `AppModule`, it's just a regular class and Nest throws a fit when you try to inject it. Duh. Makes total sense now why my `UsersService` kept breaking before.
