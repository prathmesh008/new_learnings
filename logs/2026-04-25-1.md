## 2026-04-25 — async/await under the hood

Okay so `async/await` under the hood finally clicked for real today, kinda tripped me up for ages. It's not actually *blocking* the thread when you `await` something, especially in JS. That `await` just tells the runtime, 'yo, pause *this specific function* and put the rest of its execution into a `.then()` callback that'll run when the promise resolves.' The event loop just keeps on doing its thing, running other tasks. My mind was like, `async function getData() { console.log('1'); await somePromise(); console.log('2'); }` - that `console.log('2')` is basically inside a hidden `.then()` call. Makes so much more sense now.
